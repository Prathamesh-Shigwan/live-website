Ocean Recovery

How We Turned 4.2 Million Kilograms of Ocean Plastic Into Products You Use Every Day

In 2021, we launched our most ambitious project yet — a partnership with 14 coastal communities across Southeast Asia to intercept ocean-bound plastic before it reaches the sea. Three years on, here's what we learned.

The Problem We Couldn't Ignore

By 2021, an estimated 8 million tonnes of plastic were entering the world's oceans every year. The numbers were staggering, the imagery heartbreaking. But at HitShi, we kept asking ourselves a different question: what happens to the plastic before it reaches the sea?

The answer, it turned out, was mostly nothing. Coastal communities in developing nations — particularly across Southeast Asia — were generating enormous volumes of plastic waste with no viable collection or recycling infrastructure. The plastic was either burned, dumped in waterways, or abandoned on shorelines. It was ocean-bound plastic, and nobody had figured out how to intercept it at scale.

🌊

What is ocean-bound plastic? Plastic waste located within 50km of a coastline in countries with inadequate waste management infrastructure. It's not yet in the ocean — but it will be within months without intervention. Intercepting it here is far cheaper and more effective than ocean cleanup.

Building the Collection Network

In January 2021, we dispatched our first team to three provinces in the Philippines — Eastern Samar, Leyte, and Northern Cebu. What we found was both troubling and encouraging. Troubling: communities producing hundreds of tonnes of mixed plastic monthly with nowhere for it to go. Encouraging: a deep, genuine desire among local residents to participate in a solution, if one could be made economically viable.

We piloted a simple model: community collectors — predominantly fisherfolk whose livelihoods were already threatened by polluted waters — would collect, sort, and weigh plastic waste at mobile collection points. HitShi would pay a fair price per kilogram, handle logistics, and transport material to our processing facilities in Manila and Surabaya.

The economics were tight but viable. Crucially, we weren't asking communities to donate labour for a cause — we were creating a new income stream. That distinction mattered enormously to buy-in.

We weren't asking communities to donate labour for a cause. We were creating a dignified, permanent income stream — and that changed everything.

— Dr. Leila Hassan, Co-Founder & CEO

The Processing Challenge

Ocean-bound plastic presents distinct challenges compared to post-industrial waste. It's heavily contaminated, mixed across resin types, and often degraded by UV exposure and moisture. Our existing processing lines were designed for cleaner feedstock. We needed to rethink.

Over 18 months and three iterations, our engineering team — led by Prof. Nkosi Okafor — developed what we call our Cascade Wash Protocol: a seven-stage cleaning and separation process that brings heavily contaminated ocean-bound material up to the same purity standards as post-industrial feedstock.

Key innovations in the protocol:

  • Enzymatic pre-treatment — bio-derived enzymes break down biofilm and organic contamination before mechanical washing
  • Density-cascade separation — five sequential float-sink tanks separate resin types with 99.3% accuracy
  • UV-degradation repair — proprietary additive package that restores mechanical properties lost to weathering
  • Closed-loop water recycling — 98.6% of process water is recovered and re-used

From Ocean to Your Garden

Today, ocean-bound rHDPE from our Southeast Asian network forms part of the feedstock for our RecyDeck Lumber, GreenBench Park Bench, and EcoFence Panels. Every unit is stamped with our Ocean Recovered certification and includes a QR code that traces the material back to its country of origin.

For our B2B partners, this matters significantly. The EU's Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR), which entered into force in 2024, includes specific provisions crediting ocean-bound plastic recovery with a 1.5× recycled content multiplier. Brands using our certified ocean-bound rHDPE can claim compliance advantages that standard recycled plastic cannot provide.

🏷️

Certification note: Our ocean-bound plastic supply chain is verified by Vericel Ocean Bound Plastic (OBP Standard 3.0) and DNV GL. Third-party audits are conducted annually. Full chain-of-custody documentation available on request for all B2B partners.

What We Got Wrong

In the spirit of honest reporting, it's worth noting where we stumbled. Our initial pricing model underestimated logistics costs by around 30%. We had to renegotiate with our processing partners in year two, which caused a painful six-week disruption to collections.

We also underestimated the importance of community trust-building. In two provinces, collections dropped sharply after a payments delay caused by banking infrastructure issues. It took months of on-the-ground relationship repair to recover momentum. We've since moved to weekly mobile payments and maintain a 60-day liquidity reserve specifically to ensure collector payments are never delayed.

What's Next

In 2026, we're expanding the network into Bangladesh and coastal Tanzania, targeting an additional 500,000 kg of annual collection capacity. We're also launching our HitShi Collector App — a mobile platform that allows collectors to log collections, track earnings, and access training materials offline.

The goal by 2028: 10 million kg recovered, 1,000 active collectors, 25 communities. We believe it's achievable. We're going to prove it.
